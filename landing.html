Ngāti Raukawa first settled in Ōtaki in 1820, attributing their values and customs to manage the river. This changed in 1840 when the Treaty of Waitangi was signed allowing English law to clear the floodplain for agricultural use. The Ōtaki River was then managed by the Hutt River Board (1924) and later by the Manawatu Catchment Board (1943). Since 1989, the Ōtaki River has predominantly been managed by the Greater Wellington Regional Council alongside the Kapiti Coast District Council(Royal, 1992).
